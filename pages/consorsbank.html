Consorsbank - Consumer Loans

Goal

The loan calculator and the application process for the Consorsbank consumer loan should be made more user-friendly. The product was completely overhauled using the UX design process.

My Role

I accompanied the project shortly after kick-off and was mainly responsible for the conception, prototyping and final design.

Used Tools

Pen & paper (storyboard), Photoshop (concept wireframes & design), Axure (prototyping)

Storyboards

At the beginning of the project, storyboards for various personas were developed. The aim of the storyboards was to present the functions and possibilities of consumer credit and to illustrate them with a simple story.

One of these storyboards shows the story of Max, who wants to use the loan to finance a new bathroom. Using simple sketches, the first design possibilities for the later product are shown.

Concept

In the concept I was mainly occupied with the development of the loan calculator, as this is the core of the application process. The user-friendliness of the credit calculator is crucial in determining whether and how the user enters the application process.

Once the user had decided on a credit line, they entered the application process. Since this consists of many input fields, these were not only divided into two pages (personal data, financial data) but also separated into specific areas (personal data, contact data, employment / monthly income, expenses, account data).

Prototypes

Three different prototypes were made using Axure to find out wich calculator works best for the user.

Loan Calculator 1

Since the loan calculator is the entry point to the application process, it should be color-coded to stand out from the rest of the page at the beginning. Although users quickly became aware of the calculator, they found it too intrusive.

Loan Calculator 2

In the second variant, the color was taken back a little and should only be used as a highlight for data. Nevertheless, the displayed information was still too much for the user, the chart was completely superfluous.

Loan Calculator 3

For the third and final variant, all unnecessary information was therefore removed and only credit-relevant data was presented.

Application Process

In addition to the loan calculator, the application process with data input and the conclusion of the consumer loan was also tested. Especially the expectations regarding the input of personal data and the general duration of the application were tested.

Design

After the last iteration, the test results were implemented and wrapped in the appropriate Consorsbank apparel. In the design, care was taken to ensure that input fields are clearly recognizable as such, that all relevant information is visible at a glance and that the user is transparently informed at which point of the application process he is currently at.
