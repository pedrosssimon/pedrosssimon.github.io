Deutsche Bahn - Career Portal

Goal

The career portal of Deutsche Bahn should be revamped to find the right job for pupils, specialists and academics. Focus groups and co-creation workshops were used to determine the needs of the individual target groups. These were then transferred into a concept and tested in user tests.

My Role

I accompanied the project during the co-creation workshops, the conception phase and testing.

Used Tools

Pen & paper (workshops), Sketch (concept, wireframes & design), InVision (prototyping)

Co-Creation

Workshops

In order to know the needs for a career portal of the three target groups (students, professionals, academics), a co-creation workshop was conducted for each target group. Participants were asked to describe the perfect application process for them. From the initial idea of the job search to the positive or negative feedback from the employer.

The workshop participants then used existing job advertisements to create the ideal job structure for them. Ideas from this were then incorporated into the concept.

Customer Journey

The results of the co-creation resulted in a Customer Journey for the application process, which accompanied us throughout the entire course of the project and was always a point of reference for further design.

Concept

Derived from the workshops and the Customer Journey, "How might we" questions were formulated to create the concept of the career portal. The focus in the concept was clearly on searching or finding the right job and a job offer with relevant content.

How might we...

... help (particularly students) to find the right job?

... integrate all relevant information into the job description?

... simplify the transition from mobile to desktop during the process?

... provide real insights into everyday working life and the working environment?

The search should therefore be carried out in three steps in order to display the best possible results after the first search query. Dynamic filters, depending on the primary data, can be used to further specify the results.

Prototype

User Test

For the test scenario, users from two target groups (students, skilled workers) should search for a desired job, favour it and then prepare the job application on desktop. (Test stopped when clicking on "Bewerbung vorbereiten")

Connect Favourites With Code

During the test it turned out that the users had no difficulties with the three-part search and were satisfied with the presentation of the jobs they were looking for. For many of them the linking of the favorites via the code was not intuitive, but the function without registration or entering data was very popular.

Job Offer

For the job offer, the skilled workers in particular wanted more concrete information about the job. The events offered by the employer were particularly well received by pupils.
