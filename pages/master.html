Driver Less - Master Thesis

Research Questions

Question 1: How can both parties - driver and intelligent vehicle - grow together to form a team so that the driving task can be mastered in the best possible way in stressful situations?

Question 2: How can driver and vehicle communicate with each other - via the interface available in the vehicle (voice, displays, light & haptics) - and how is this communication designed?

Used Tools

Cultural probes (research), pen & paper (storyboard & sketching), Sketch (concept & design), paper and cardboard (prototyping)

Cultural Probes

In order to familiarize myself with the topic of the work, I started by looking at comparable work, the functions and possibilities of semi-autonomous vehicles and the development of stress in cars.

Since the target group was not yet clearly defined at the beginning of the work, I decided to conduct Cultural Probes. These served to understand the target group and their relationship to their own vehicle, driving behaviour and stressful situations in the car.

Six people took part in the diary study. They had one week to answer the questions of the study and fill in the diary over five days. The diary studies also covered the aspect of Thing Centered Design. The participants' vehicles are seen as humanized and intelligent objects.

Personas

From the results of the cultural probes, personas for the human users and object personas for their vehicles can be generated for the master thesis. In total, three human personas and three object personas are created. The object personas are based on the results of the Thing Centered Design section of the cultural probes.

The personas are composed of pragmatic and passionate drivers. These are Karin, Tim and Werner.

The object personas represent three vehicles and are partners matching the human personas. The vehicles are also given human traits. The object personas are Henry, Lise and Matteo.

Scenarios

Starting with the personas and their vehicles, they are combined in scenarios (Karin and Henry, Tim and Lise, Werner and Matteo). The scenarios are not specifically based on the results of the Cultural Probes but are inspired by them.

The goal of the scenarios is to uncover unpleasant situations while driving in a car and to find appropriate answers. Thus all three personas struggle with a feeling of stress and discomfort in the car.

Scenario 1: Snow Track

In the first scenario, the stress for the person in the vehicle is caused by weather influences from outside. Dense snow and a slippery, snow-covered road surface make the driving task a challenge.

Scenario 2: The Way Home

The stress situation for the driver in the second scenario is caused by evening rush hour traffic and road works in the city.

Scenario 3: Customer Meeting

An impending customer meeting and heavy traffic create a stressful situation in the third scenario, as the driver is nervous and constantly distracted from the task of driving.

Ideation

After creating scenarios from the personas, I brainstormed and scribbled to the problems. The sketches were used for the idea development. For each scenario several solutions were scribbled.

At the same time, goals were defined for each scenario in order to guide the approaches towards a specific goal. Some of the ideas are presented here:

Light

In the first scenario the bad weather on the mountain road makes Karin's life difficult. The car slides on the slippery road and visibility requires concentration and driving skills.

Goal: Karin arrives safely, stress-free and without too much effort at the end of the pass road.

A camera in the car and sensors on the steering wheel detect Karin's stress level. She slides excitedly back and forth in her seat. Her hands become damp from the sweat production in view of the challenge on the track. If the system in the vehicle perceives Karin as stressed in the face of the bad weather conditions on the mountain pass road, it adjusts the lighting mood in the vehicle and changes to a bluish, soothing light tone.

Route Planning

On the way home after work, Tim wants to get through the rush hour traffic in a relaxed manner and reach his home without stress. He especially wants to avoid the many road works or traffic jams.

Goal: Tim arrives at home relaxed and without any major detours after a strenuous day at work.

As soon as Tim gets into the car, the intelligent vehicle has already determined the best possible route for the most relaxed journey home. The system does not necessarily take into account the fastest route, but pays attention to road works, closed roads or increased traffic. Based on these criteria, the vehicle automatically determines the upcoming route.

Call for Concentration

On the way to a customer meeting, Werner is repeatedly distracted by thoughts and therefore cannot concentrate on the road. His thoughts are already at the customer appointment. His vehicle notices that something is wrong and supports him.

Goal: Werner arrives at his customer meeting stress-free and prepared.

As the driver's opponent, the semi-autonomous vehicle talks to Werner and challenges him to concentrate. Friction is created between the two parties in the vehicle (friction). Werner follows the commands of the vehicle, concentrates on the road and reaches his destination faster.

Concept

In order to refine this work to a scenario, I held a workshop after the idea generation, which served to evaluate ideas and commit to a scenario. At the end of the workshop the focus was on two scenarios. I then decided on Scenario 1 "Snow Track" because I found the context for this master thesis more exciting and the possibilities for solutions more versatile.

The scenario is presented by means of four close ups along the journey. These close ups are determined using the following graphs. These graphs show Karin's stress level, the driving challenge and the intervention of the semi-autonomous vehicle. Towards the end of the scenario all three graphs increase and culminate in close up 3.

The interfaces for the design of this master thesis are presented using the following interfaces.

Close Up 1

In the first close-up Karin's stress level rises slightly. The vehicle senses the change and automatically changes the lighting mood in the vehicle. From a warm yellow the lighting mood changes to a blue tone. The semi-autonomous vehicle prevents Karin's stress development and can even lower the stress level slightly. All this happens inconspicuously, without the driver's intervention, in the background.

Close Up 2

In the second close-up Karin is already on the mountain road for a while. The weather is getting worse and worse, it starts to snow and her stress level continues to rise despite the pleasant light atmosphere.

The system offers Karin to help her navigate the snowy road. Karin accepts the offer. The semi-autonomous vehicle gives instructions and assistance in navigation to help Karin manoeuvre through the poor visibility and weather conditions. Forward curves are described by the system and hints are given about sections of the road that are difficult to see.

Close Up 3

While Karin is still making good progress on the pass road, vehicles in front report that visibility is getting worse and the snowfall is getting denser.

The semi-autonomous vehicle explains the circumstances and offers to switch to the autonomous mode, as it considers this step to be the most useful for a safe and relaxed continuation of the journey.

Karin agrees with the proposal. The semi-autonomous vehicle confirms the input and declares that the autonomous operation will be activated in five seconds. The system will count down the seconds. Karin keeps her hands on the steering wheel but moves her feet back from the pedals.

The vehicle accelerates, brakes and steers autonomously. Karin continues to hold the steering wheel and is relaxed by the slowing vibration of the steering wheel and the pulsating light.

The surface of the dashboard has changed. The accent color of the user interface changes from orange to blue to symbolize the change from controlled to autonomous.

The autonomous mode makes Karin relax and her stress level decreases.

Close Up 4

This close-up shows the multimodality of the interface. Karin does not necessarily always have to communicate with Henry to switch to the desired driving mode. In some situations the vehicle should react to the driver's motor input.

Karin grips the steering wheel more firmly, feels the pulsating vibration in her hands. The vehicle is still in autonomous driving mode. She places her feet above the pedals again and presses the accelerator pedal. Karin overrides the autonomous mode of the car and is now the steering unit again. The driver has priority when entering commands and stands above the autonomous system. The vibration in the steering wheel stops, the light in the car stops pulsing. The interface immediately changes from the autonomous mode back to the controlled mode.

Another possibility for transferring the driving task back to Karin would be to use voice control. Karin simply asks Henry if she can cover the last few kilometers of the route. Henry agrees and initiates the transfer. Karin positions her feet above the pedals again and waits for the signal that this will end the autonomous ride.

Paper Prototype

For the presentation of the master thesis I built a prototype of the vehicle interface. It shows with an animation how the transition from the steered mode to the autonomous mode, which takes place in Close Up 3, works. Additionally the prototype shows how the pulsating blue light in the vehicle can contribute to calming down.

For the construction of the prototype I use two laptops over which the built vehicle interior can be slid. Underneath are the animations for the dashboard and the center screen. I derived the animations from the already existing interfaces. I disassembled them into their individual parts and put them back together in Premiere Pro to animate them.

The goal of the prototype is to present the designed interfaces in the context of a low-fidelity car interior. Thereby the interfaces created in the work should be brought into a realistic environment.
